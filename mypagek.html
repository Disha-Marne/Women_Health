Menstruation

Menstruation (periods) is the shedding of the uterine lining, occurring approximately every 28 days. It marks the start of puberty in girls, usually between 10-15 years.

What Happens During a Period?

  • The uterus prepares a lining for a possible pregnancy.
  • If pregnancy doesn’t happen, the lining sheds as blood.
  • The cycle repeats every 21-35 days, lasting 2-7 days.

Common Issues

  • Irregular Periods – It’s normal in the first few years as the body adjusts.
  • Menstrual Cramps (Dysmenorrhea) – Pain due to uterus contractions.
  • Fatigue & Weakness – Loss of iron and changes in hormones.
  • Heavy or Light Flow – Flow amount may vary, but extreme changes should be checked.
  • Premenstrual Syndrome – Symptoms like bloating, mood swings, and headache before period triggered by hormonal fluctuations.
