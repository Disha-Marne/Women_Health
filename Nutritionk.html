Nutritional Needs and Deficiencies

Anemia is a condition where the body does not have enough healthy red blood cells (RBCs) to carry oxygen to tissues. This leads to fatigue, weakness, dizziness, and poor concentration, especially in growing adolescents.

Signs of Nutritional Deficiencies

  • Iron Deficiency (Anemia) –Symptoms: Fatigue, pale skin, dizziness, shortness of breath, frequent headaches
      Causes: Low iron intake, heavy menstruation, lack of iron absorption
      Solution: Eat more spinach, lentils, red meat, fortified cereals, nuts
  • Calcium & Vitamin D Deficiency – Symptoms: Weak bones, delayed growth, muscle cramps, dental problems
      Causes: Lack of dairy intake, minimal sun exposure
      Solution: Increase intake of milk, yogurt, cheese, almonds, fortified cereals, sunlight exposure
  • Protein Deficiency – Symptoms: Slow growth, weak muscles, hair thinning, constant fatigue.
      Causes: Insufficient protein intake, poor digestion
      Solution: Eat more pulses, eggs, lean meat, dairy, tofu, nuts.
  • Zinc & Magnesium Deficiency – Symptoms: Frequent colds, brittle nails, hair loss, sleep disturbances
      Causes: Poor diet, high processed food intake.
      Solution: Eat more nuts, seeds, whole grains, leafy greens, dairy.
  • Vitamin A Deficiency – Symptoms: Poor vision (especially at night), dry skin, weak immunity
      Causes: Poor diet, lack of fruit and vegetable intake
      Solution: Eat more carrots, sweet potatoes, papaya, fish, eggs, dairy
