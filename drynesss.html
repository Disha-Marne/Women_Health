Vaginal Dryness

Vaginal dryness is a common symptom experienced by many women during and after menopause. It occurs due to declining levels of estrogen, which leads to thinning and drying of the vaginal walls. This can result in discomfort, itching and burning sensations.

Here are some approaches that can help manage vaginal dryness:

  • Maintain Hygiene: Avoid harsh soaps, douches, and scented products in the vaginal area, as these can exacerbate dryness. Opt for gentle, fragrance-free cleansers.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drinking plenty of water can help maintain overall hydration, which can benefit vaginal moisture levels as well.
  • Healthy Diet: Consuming foods rich in phytoestrogens, such as soy products, flaxseeds, and whole grains, may help support estrogen levels naturally.
  • Pelvic Floor Exercises: Kegel exercises can help improve pelvic floor muscle tone and circulation, which may support vaginal health.
