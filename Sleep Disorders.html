Sleep Disorders & Insomnia

Sleep disturbances are common in older adults due to changes in sleep patterns, hormonal shifts, medical conditions, and lifestyle factors. Many elderly individuals experience difficulty falling asleep, frequent night awakenings, or poor sleep quality, leading to fatigue, irritability, and increased health risks. Proper sleep hygiene, a healthy lifestyle, and medical management can help improve sleep and overall well-being.






Common Sleep Disorders

  • Insomnia (Difficulty Falling or Staying Asleep) Caused by stress, anxiety, medication side effects, or chronic pain. Leads to daytime sleepiness, poor concentration, and mood swings.
  • Sleep Apnea (Interrupted Breathing During Sleep) – A condition where breathing stops multiple times during sleep, reducing oxygen levels. Symptoms: Loud snoring, gasping for air, headaches, and excessive daytime sleepiness. Increases the risk of heart disease and high blood pressure.
  • Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS) – Causes an uncontrollable urge to move the legs, especially at night. Leads to difficulty falling asleep and disrupted rest.
  • Circadian Rhythm Changes (Altered Sleep-Wake Cycle) - Aging shifts sleep schedules, causing early bedtime and early morning awakenings. Can lead to fatigue and decreased energy levels during the day.
  • Frequent Night Awakenings & Light Sleep - Older adults spend less time in deep sleep, making them more sensitive to noise, discomfort, or temperature changes.

Ways to Improve Sleep

  • Maintain a Regular Sleep Schedule – Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day.
  • Create a Relaxing Bedtime Routine – Read, listen to soft music, or practice meditation before bed.
  • Limit Screen Time Before Sleep – Avoid using phones, TVs, and computers at least an hour before bed.
  • Avoid Caffeine & Heavy Meals at Night- Stimulants like tea, coffee, and spicy foods disrupt sleep.
  • Limit Daytime Naps- If necessary, keep naps short (20-30 minutes) to avoid nighttime sleep disruptions.
