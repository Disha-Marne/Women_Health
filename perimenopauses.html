HORMONAL SHIFT AND PERIMENOPAUSE

Perimenopause is the transitional phase before menopause, typically occurring in women in their 40s but sometimes starting in their late 30s. It can last anywhere from a few years to over a decade. During this phase, the ovaries gradually produce less estrogen, leading to irregular menstrual cycles and various physical and emotional symptoms. Once a woman goes 12 consecutive months without a period, she is considered to have reached menopause.

Hormonal Changes During Perimenopause
  • Declining Estrogen Levels: The primary hormonal shift, leading to irregular periods, mood swings, and changes in skin and metabolism.
  • Fluctuations in Progesterone: Can cause sleep disturbances, anxiety, and heavier or lighter periods.
  • Changes in Testosterone: May lead to reduced libido, fatigue, and muscle mass loss.
  • Increased Cortisol Sensitivity: Heightened stress response, leading to weight gain and anxiety.
  • Insulin Resistance: Some women may experience blood sugar fluctuations, increasing the risk of weight gain and metabolic issues.
