OSTEOPOROSIS

As estrogen level falls, the calcium gets sucked out of the bones to maintain blood calcium levels as there is low amount of calcium intake in diet.
This causes weakening of bones leading to bone pain which presents as backache in postmenopausal females.
Because the bones are weaker, even a minor fall or bump can cause a fracture. Keeping bones strong with a healthy diet, exercise, and sometimes medications can help prevent or manage osteoporosis.
That is why in postmenopausal females,calcium rich diet is necessary.

Ignorance towards it can lead to fractures like :

  • Vertebral compression fracture
  • Tooth loss
  • Loss of height: Compression fractures in the spine can cause a person to become shorter over time.
  • Back pain: Fractures in the spinal vertebrae can lead to chronic back pain.
  • Poor posture: Spinal fractures can cause a stooped or hunched posture, known as kyphosis.
  • Limited mobility: Fractures and pain can make it difficult to move around, affecting daily activities.

Preventing and treating osteoporosis through diet, exercise, and medication can help minimize these effects.
