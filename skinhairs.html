SKIN AND HAIR CHANGES

  1. Acne & Oily Skin
    • PCOS: High androgen (male hormone) levels cause excess oil production, leading to persistent acne (especially on the jawline, chin, and cheeks).
    • Menstrual Cycle: Some women experience cyclical breakouts before their period due to progesterone and estrogen dips.
    • Perimenopause: Fluctuating hormones may cause adult-onset acne, even in women who never had acne before.
  2. Dryness & Sensitivity
    • Menopause: Low estrogen reduces collagen and oil production, making skin dry, thin, and more prone to irritation.
    • PCOS: Insulin resistance can lead to patchy, dull skin.
  3. Hyperpigmentation & Dark Spots
    • PCOS & Insulin Resistance: PCOS & Insulin Resistance:
    • Sun Sensitivity (Menopause): Estrogen decline reduces skin protection, making it more prone to sunspots and melasma.
  4. Hair Thinning & Hair Loss
    • Menopause & Perimenopause: Lower estrogen levels lead to weaker hair follicles, causing hair thinning, receding hairline, and reduced volume.
    • PCOS: High androgen levels trigger hair loss at the crown and temples.
  5. Excess Facial & Body Hair
    • High androgens (PCOS) cause thick, coarse hair to grow on the chin, upper lip, chest, and back.
  6. Brittle & Dry Hair
    • Estrogen decline (Menopause) leads to weaker, brittle strands prone to breakage.
