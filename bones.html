Bone Density & Joint Health

  1. Bone Density Loss (Osteoporosis Risk)
    • Estrogen Decline (Menopause & Perimenopause): Estrogen is crucial for maintaining bone strength. Its reduction leads to calcium loss, brittle bones, and a higher risk of fractures.
    • PCOS & Insulin Resistance: Women with PCOS often have low Vitamin D and calcium absorption issues, weakening bones.
    • Menstrual Irregularities: Women with infrequent periods (PCOS or early menopause) may experience reduced bone mineral density over time.
  2. Pain & Stiffness
    • Inflammation from Hormonal Imbalances: Low estrogen increases inflammation, contributing to joint pain, stiffness, and swelling (similar to arthritis).
    • PCOS-Related Weight Gain: Excess weight puts pressure on joints, leading to discomfort, especially in the knees and lower back.
    • Menstrual-Related Water Retention: Some women experience temporary joint pain and bloating before or during their periods.
  3. Weakness & Fatigue
    • Menopause & PCOS affect muscle mass, leading to weakness, aches, and slower recovery from physical activities.
    • Reduced Collagen Production: Estrogen helps in collagen formation, and its decline affects joint flexibility and skin elasticity.
