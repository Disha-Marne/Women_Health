SLEEP DISRUPTION

  1. Hormonal Imbalances & Insomnia
    • Declining estrogen & progesterone levels make it harder to fall and stay asleep.
    • Lower melatonin production affects the body's natural sleep cycle.
    • High insulin and cortisol levels disturb sleep patterns, leading to restlessness and difficulty falling asleep.
    • Progesterone dips before menstruation can lead to insomnia or restless sleep.
  2. Night Sweats & Hot Flashes (Menopause & Perimenopause)
    • Sudden body temperature fluctuations cause night sweats, leading to frequent awakenings.
  3. Anxiety & Stress (Common in PCOS & Menopause)
    • Increased cortisol (stress hormone) levels contribute to racing thoughts and difficulty unwinding.
  4. Sleep Apnea & Snoring (Linked to PCOS & Menopause)
    • PCOS increases the risk of sleep apnea, where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep.
    • Weight gain (common in PCOS & menopause) can contribute to snoring and poor sleep quality.

Ways to Improve Sleep & Prevent Insomnia

  • Follow a Consistent Sleep Schedule – Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily, even on weekends.
  • Create a Relaxing Bedtime Routine – Wind down with activities like reading, meditation, or a warm bath.
  • Limit Screen Time Before Bed – Avoid phones, TVs, and laptops at least an hour before sleeping.
  • Reduce Caffeine & Alcohol Intake – Avoid stimulants in the evening to prevent sleep disturbances.
  • Take Breaks at Work – Short breaks throughout the day can improve focus and prevent burnout.
  • Exercise Regularly – Engaging in physical activity during the day promotes better sleep.
