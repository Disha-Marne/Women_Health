Early Puberty and Growth Issues

Early puberty, also called precocious puberty, happens when a girl's body starts showing signs of puberty before age 8. Normally, puberty starts between ages 9-14, but some girls experience it earlier due to hormonal imbalances, genetics, or environmental factors.

Signs of Early Puberty

Breast Development and Growth Spurts
Body Odor due to Excess Sweat
Mood Swings
Acne and Oily Skin Issues

What Affects Puberty & Growth?

  • Hormones

    Puberty is triggered by estrogen and other hormones that affect body growth. If these hormones are released early, a girl may grow faster initially but stop growing sooner, leading to a shorter adult height.

  • Genetics

    If a mother had early puberty, her daughter might experience the same. However, lifestyle and diet can also play a role.

  • Diet & Nutrition

    Unhealthy diets high in processed foods, sugar, and fats can lead to obesity, which is linked to early puberty.

  • Environmental Factors

    Endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) in plastics, cosmetics, and food packaging can interfere with hormones.
