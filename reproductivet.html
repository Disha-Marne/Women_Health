Reproductive Health Issues

Reproductive health issues refer to problems affecting the reproductive system, including hormonal imbalances, infections, fertility challenges, and conditions like PCOS, endometriosis, and fibroids. These issues can impact menstruation, pregnancy, and overall well-being.

Various Reproductive Health Issues

  • PCOS – A hormonal disorder where high androgen levels cause irregular periods, excessive hair growth, weight gain, and fertility issues. It can also increase the risk of diabetes and heart disease if left untreated.

  • Fibroids – Non-cancerous tumors in the uterus that vary in size and may cause heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, frequent urination, and complications during pregnancy.

  • Endometriosis: A painful condition where tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus, leading to inflammation, severe cramps, heavy periods, and potential infertility.

  • Infertility: The inability to conceive due to hormonal imbalances, structural abnormalities (like blocked fallopian tubes or fibroids), ovulation disorders, or lifestyle factors such as stress and poor nutrition.

  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID): A serious infection of the female reproductive organs, usually caused by untreated STIs. It can lead to chronic pelvic pain, abnormal discharge, fever, and long-term fertility issues if not treated promptly.
