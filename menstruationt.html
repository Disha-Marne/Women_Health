Menstruation

Menstruation is the monthly shedding of the uterine lining (endometrium) through the vagina. It occurs as part of the menstrual cycle when pregnancy does not happen. Typically lasting 3-7 days, menstruation involves blood and tissue discharge from the uterus. It is regulated by hormonal changes, primarily estrogen and progesterone, and usually begins during puberty (menarche) and ends at menopause.






Various Menstruation Problems

  • Irregular Periods – Unpredictable cycles due to stress, hormones, or underlying conditions.
  • Painful Periods (Dysmenorrhea) – Cramping caused by uterine contractions, often linked to hormonal imbalances.
  • Heavy Menstrual Bleeding (Menorrhagia) – Excessive bleeding that may indicate hormonal issues, fibroids, or other health concerns.
  • Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS) – Symptoms like bloating, mood swings, and headaches before periods, triggered by hormonal fluctuations.
