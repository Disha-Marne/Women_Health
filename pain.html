Weakening Bones & Joint Pain

As people age, bone density naturally decreases, making bones more fragile and increasing the risk of fractures. Joints also experience wear and tear, leading to stiffness, pain, and reduced mobility. Conditions like osteoporosis (weak bones) and arthritis (joint inflammation) are common among the elderly, affecting daily activities and overall quality of life. Proper nutrition, regular movement, and medical care can help maintain bone strength and joint health.






Common Bone & Joint Problems

  • Osteoporosis (Weak & Brittle Bones) Loss of bone density makes bones fragile and prone to fractures. More common in postmenopausal women due to lower estrogen levels. Fractures often occur in the hip, spine, and wrist.
  • Arthritis (Joint Inflammation & Stiffness) – Osteoarthritis (OA): Caused by wear and tear of joints over time, leading to pain and stiffness. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA): An autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks joint tissues, causing swelling and pain.
  • Joint Pain & Stiffness – Cartilage (the cushion between bones) wears down, causing friction and pain in joints. Most commonly affects knees, hips, shoulders, and hands.
  • Fractures & Risk of FallsWeak bones increase the risk of fractures from minor falls. Hip fractures are particularly dangerous in old age, leading to mobility issues.
  • Loss of Height & Posture ChangesSpinal compression or osteoporosis can cause height loss and a hunched posture. Can lead to back pain and difficulty in movement.

Ways to Maintain Bone & Joint Health

  • Increase Calcium & Vitamin D Intake – Dairy products, leafy greens, and sunlight exposure help strengthen bones.
  • Use Joint-Friendly Movements – Avoid sudden movements or heavy lifting to prevent joint strain.
  • Maintain a Healthy Weight – Reduces excess pressure on joints, especially knees and hips.
  • Use Supportive Footwear & Walking Aids-Helps with stability and reduces the risk of falls.
  • Seek Medical Advice if Pain Persists-Severe pain or reduced mobility may require physiotherapy or medical intervention.
