Insomnia

Insomnia, or difficulty falling or staying asleep, is a common issue among women in their 20s and 30s. Factors such as stress, hormonal fluctuations, lifestyle habits, and underlying health conditions can disrupt sleep patterns. Poor sleep quality affects energy levels, mood, concentration, and overall health. Identifying the root causes and adopting better sleep habits can significantly improve rest and well-being.






Common Causes of Insomnia

  • Stress & Anxiety – Overthinking, work pressure, financial worries, and relationship issues can make it difficult to relax and fall asleep. Chronic stress increases cortisol levels, disrupting the body's natural sleep-wake cycle.
  • Hormonal Changes – Menstrual cycle fluctuations can cause sleep disturbances, especially before periods (PMS). Pregnancy and postpartum hormone shifts can lead to insomnia. Thyroid disorders (hyperthyroidism) can cause restlessness and difficulty sleeping.
  • Poor Sleep Habits & Lifestyle Choices – Irregular sleep schedules (sleeping late, inconsistent bedtimes) disrupt the body's circadian rhythm. Excessive screen time before bed (phones, laptops) reduces melatonin, the sleep hormone.
  • Caffeine, Alcohol, & Poor Diet – Drinking coffee, tea, or energy drinks late in the day can keep you awake at night. Alcohol may initially induce drowsiness but disrupts deep sleep cycles. A high-sugar or heavy meal close to bedtime can lead to discomfort and restlessness.
  • Overstimulation Before Bedtime – Engaging in intense work, social media scrolling, or watching action-packed TV shows before bed can keep the brain active and delay sleep.
  • Sleep Disorders (Restless Leg Syndrome, Sleep Apnea) – Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS) causes an uncontrollable urge to move the legs, making sleep difficult. Sleep apnea leads to breathing interruptions during sleep, causing frequent wake-ups.
  • Pregnancy & Postpartum Sleep Issues – Physical discomfort, frequent urination, and hormonal shifts can make sleep difficult during pregnancy. New mothers often experience disrupted sleep patterns due to newborn care.

Ways to Improve Sleep & Prevent Insomnia

  • Follow a Consistent Sleep Schedule – Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily, even on weekends.
  • Create a Relaxing Bedtime Routine – Wind down with activities like reading, meditation, or a warm bath.
  • Limit Screen Time Before Bed – Avoid phones, TVs, and laptops at least an hour before sleeping.
  • Reduce Caffeine & Alcohol Intake – Avoid stimulants in the evening to prevent sleep disturbances.
  • Take Breaks at Work – Short breaks throughout the day can improve focus and prevent burnout.
  • Exercise Regularly – Engaging in physical activity during the day promotes better sleep.
