PCOS/PCOD

  • PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) – PCOS is a hormonal disorder causing irregular periods, weight gain, excess hair growth, and infertility, with risks of diabetes and heart disease.

  • PCOD (Polycystic Ovarian Disease) – PCOD is a condition where the ovaries produce immature eggs, causing hormonal imbalances, irregular periods, and mild symptoms. It is usually manageable with lifestyle changes.

Various PCOS/PCOD Problems

  • Hormonal Imbalance: PCOS causes increased androgen levels leading to irregular periods, acne, weight gain, and excessive facial or body hair. Some women may also experience scalp hair thinning.

  • Cysts in Ovaries: Small fluid-filled sacs in the ovaries prevent proper egg release, leading to irregular menstrual cycles and potential fertility issues. These cysts are often painless but disrupt ovulation.

  • Insulin Resistance: The body struggles to use insulin effectively, causing weight gain and increasing the risk of Type 2 diabetes. It can also lead to dark skin patches (Acanthosis Nigricans) around the neck and armpits.

  • Infertility Risk: Irregular or absent ovulation makes it difficult to conceive.PCOS can also increase the risk of pregnancy complications, but lifestyle changes and medical treatments can help improve fertility.
