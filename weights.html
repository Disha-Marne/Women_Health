Metabolism and Weight Changes

  1. Hormonal Imbalances & Metabolism Slowdown Menopause & Perimenopause:
    • Lower estrogen levels cause fat to accumulate around the abdomen (instead of hips and thighs).
    • Metabolism slows down, making weight loss harder.
    • Higher insulin levels make the body store more fat, leading to weight gain, especially around the belly.
    • Many women with PCOS struggle with losing weight despite diet and exercise.
    • Progesterone fluctuations before menstruation cause bloating, temporary weight gain, and cravings.
  2. Increased Cravings & Emotional Eating
    • Low serotonin levels (before periods & during menopause) trigger sugar and carb cravings.
    • PCOS-related insulin spikes lead to hunger pangs and binge eating tendencies.
  3. Muscle Loss & Fat Gain
    • Estrogen loss (menopause & PCOS) reduces muscle mass, leading to more fat accumulation.
    • Less muscle means fewer calories burned at rest, making weight gain more likely.
  4. Stress & Sleep Disruptions
    • Cortisol (stress hormone) levels rise due to hormonal fluctuations, leading to belly fat storage.
    • Sleep disturbances (common in menopause & PCOS) disrupt metabolism and increase cravings.
