Menstrual Health

Menstrual health plays a vital role in a woman’s overall well-being, especially between the ages of 20 and 35, when hormonal fluctuations, lifestyle changes, and reproductive health concerns are most prominent. A well-balanced menstrual cycle indicates good health, while irregularities can signal underlying issues.






Various Menstrual Health Problems

  • Missed Periods (Amenorrhea) – If periods stop for several months (excluding pregnancy), it could be due to excessive exercise, low body weight, stress, or medical conditions.
  • Endometriosis – A condition where tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus, causing severe pain, heavy bleeding, and sometimes fertility issues.
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) – A hormonal disorder that causes irregular periods, excess hair growth, acne, and weight gain due to high androgen levels.
  • Heavy Periods – Bleeding that lasts more than seven days or requires frequent pad/tampon changes may be due to fibroids, endometriosis, hormonal imbalances, or clotting disorders.
