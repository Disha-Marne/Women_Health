MENTAL HEALTH PROBLEMS

1. Depression & Anxiety

Estrogen fluctuations (in menopause, PCOS, and menstruation) affect serotonin and dopamine, increasing the risk of depression and anxiety.

PCOS-related insulin resistance may contribute to mood instability and stress.

Women with PMDD (severe PMS) experience intense mood swings and emotional distress.

2. Irritability & Mood Swings

Hormonal shifts cause emotional highs and lows.

Perimenopause & PCOS amplify irritability, making daily stress harder to handle.

3. Brain Fog & Memory Issues

Low estrogen levels in menopause lead to forgetfulness, difficulty concentrating, and mental fatigue.

PCOS-related insulin resistance may also affect cognitive function.

4. Sleep Disorders & Fatigue

Insomnia, night sweats (menopause), and irregular cycles (PCOS) disrupt sleep.

Poor sleep worsens anxiety, depression, and brain fog.

5. Body Image & Self-Esteem Issues

Weight gain, acne, hair loss, and excess hair growth (common in PCOS and menopause) impact confidence.

Aging changes (menopause) and bloating (menstruation) may worsen self-perception.

6. Disordered Eating & Cravings

Insulin resistance (PCOS) leads to uncontrollable cravings and risk of binge eating.

Emotional eating is common due to hormonal mood swings.
